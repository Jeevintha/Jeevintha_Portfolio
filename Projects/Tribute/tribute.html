Savitribai Phule
The First Lady of Indian Education

Here's a timeline of Savitribai Phule's life:

  • 1831 - Born in Naigaon, Maharashtra into a family of farmers belonging to the Mali community
  • 1840 - Married at the age of 9 to Jyotirao Phule, who was 13 years old
  • 1841-1847 - Educated by her husband Jyotirao at home, learns to read and write (a revolutionary act for a woman at that time)
  • 1848 - Opens the first school for girls from lower castes in Pune at Bhide Wada, becoming India's first female teacher
  • 1849 - Opens two more schools for girls and oppressed castes despite facing severe opposition and abuse from orthodox society
  • 1850 - Starts a night school for workers and peasants
  • 1851 - Sets up a care center called "Balhatya Pratibandhak Griha" for pregnant rape victims and helps deliver their children
  • 1852 - Organizes a strike of barbers to protest against the shaving of widows' heads (a cruel practice of that time)
  • 1853 - Establishes "Mahila Seva Mandal" to raise awareness about women's rights
  • 1863 - Opens a care center for drought and famine-affected people called "Balhatya Pratibandhak Griha"
  • 1868 - Allows access to her home's well for "untouchables," defying caste restrictions
  • 1873 - Leads the Satyashodhak Samaj movement founded by her husband, promoting education and social equality
  • 1876-1877 - Works tirelessly during the Great Famine, setting up 52 food distribution centers
  • 1897 - Despite being ill, sets up a clinic during the bubonic plague outbreak in Maharashtra
  • 1897 - Dies while caring for plague patients, after contracting the disease herself
